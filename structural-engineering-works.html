Overview

Structural engineering works address the load-bearing integrity of a building, work that should only be undertaken with proper engineering assessment, not guesswork. We handle column jacketing, beam strengthening, slab repair, and foundation consolidation for buildings showing signs of structural distress.

Every structural job begins with a detailed assessment: visual inspection, crack mapping, and where necessary, material testing, to understand the actual cause and extent of the issue before recommending an intervention. We do not recommend strengthening work that is not justified by the assessment, and we do not under-recommend where genuine risk exists.

This service is most commonly needed for ageing buildings, structures with visible settlement or cracking, or properties being repurposed for heavier loads than originally designed for.

Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I know if my building needs structural strengthening?

Visible signs include diagonal cracks, doors and windows that no longer close properly, visible deflection in slabs, or settlement cracks at foundation level. An engineer assessment will confirm the actual cause.

Is structural retrofitting disruptive to occupants?

It depends on the scope and location of the work. We plan execution to minimize disruption where the building remains occupied, and will be upfront if a particular intervention requires temporary vacancy.
