Overview

RCC (Reinforced Cement Concrete) roads are the right choice when a surface needs to withstand heavy and continuous loads: industrial estate access roads, housing society main roads, and commercial premises that see daily truck or heavy vehicle movement.

Siddh Sai Corporation has executed RCC road projects across Surat since the late 1990s. Our process begins with proper sub-grade preparation and compaction, because a concrete road is only as strong as the base beneath it. We then place reinforcement, shutter, and pour concrete to engineered specifications, with control joints positioned to manage thermal expansion and prevent random cracking.

Unlike bitumen, RCC roads do not soften in Surat's summer heat and resist rutting under sustained heavy loads. With proper construction, an RCC road typically outlasts a bitumen road by a significant margin, which is why most of our industrial and high-traffic clients choose this system.

Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does an RCC road take to construct?

Timeline depends on road length, width and site access, but typically 3 to 6 weeks including the mandatory curing period of at least 14 days before the road is opened to traffic.

Is RCC more expensive than bitumen roads?

RCC has a higher upfront cost than bitumen, but a significantly longer lifespan and lower long-term maintenance cost. We will walk you through the cost comparison for your specific project during the site visit.
