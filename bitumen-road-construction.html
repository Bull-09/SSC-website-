Overview

Bitumen roads remain the most common and cost-effective surface choice for housing society internal roads, private access roads, and parking areas in Surat. Done properly, with the right sub-base and drainage, a bitumen road performs reliably for 10 to 15 years even through monsoon seasons.

Most bitumen road failures we are called in to repair did not fail because of the bitumen itself, they failed because of poor sub-base preparation or inadequate drainage planning that let water collect and undermine the surface from below. We treat sub-base preparation and drainage as the most important part of the job, not an afterthought.

Our crews handle the complete scope: excavation, sub-base layering and compaction, prime coat application, bitumen surface laying at the correct temperature and thickness, and final rolling for a smooth, durable finish.

Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

Why do bitumen roads develop potholes so quickly elsewhere?

Almost always due to inadequate sub-base preparation or poor drainage, which lets water collect beneath the surface and weaken it from below. We address both before laying a single layer of bitumen.

Can bitumen roads be laid during monsoon season?

We strongly advise against it. Bitumen needs a dry sub-base and dry weather to bond and compact correctly. We schedule bitumen work outside the monsoon window for guaranteed quality.
