Sound Familiar?

You Fixed the Leak. The Contractor Left. The Rain Came Back.

Every homeowner in Surat has been through this cycle. The wall gets wet. You call a contractor. He puts something on it. It holds for one monsoon, maybe two. Then it comes back. And every time it comes back, the damage underneath has spread a little further.

The reason this happens is not bad waterproofing material. The reason is that nobody found where the water was actually coming from. Damp on your ceiling might be entering from a crack on the terrace parapet four metres away. A wet wall in your bedroom might be getting water through a construction joint on the external face of the building. Sealing the surface treats the symptom, not the source.

The Damp Patch is Never Where Water Enters

Visible dampness on your ceiling or wall can appear 3 to 6 metres away from the actual entry point. Treating the patch treats nothing.

Applying Membrane Over Wet Concrete Always Fails

If the surface is not dry, if the substrate has cracks, if the drainage is wrong — any material applied on top will fail within one to two monsoons. No exceptions.
